Integrated Population Viability Analysis (PVA): conservation portfolio evaluation


Anna Ortega
Cooperative Institute for Marine & Atmospheric Research, University of Hawaiʻi at Mānoa, Honolulu, Hawaiʻi

Zachary Siders
Fisheries and Aquatic Sciences Program, University of Florida, Gainesville, Florida

Summer Martin
Protected Species Division, Pacific Islands Fisheries Science Center, National Marine Fisheries Service, National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration, Honolulu, Hawaiʻi


Summary

Population assessments are used to inform management by quantifying the negative impact of threats or the positive impact of conservation actions on the long-term growth rate and abundance of a population. In this vignette, we examine the impacts of a multi-threat baseline (e.g., status quo fishery interactions) and evaluate five independent conservation options alongside a combined management portfolio. We estimate trends and abundance for a generic long-lived protected population (e.g., marine turtles) using a 100% self-contained, synthetic data-free pipeline. The analysis presented here builds on methods developed for two NOAA Technical Memorandums (NOAA-TM-NMFS-PIFSC-95 and NOAA-TM-NMFS-PIFSC-101), extending those foundational frameworks into a streamlined, reproducible evaluation tool.

Overview

This vignette documents an integrated Population Viability Analysis (PVA) pipeline. The goal of this tool is to assess both the impact of human-wildlife interactions (“takes”) and the efficacy of proactive conservation strategies on the long-term survival of a species. It connects three distinct modeling phases:

  1. Data Imputation: Filling gaps in historical nesting beach surveys using Bayesian state-space models via JAGS.
  2. Demographic Translation: Implementing symmetric lifecycle algebra to convert “Takes” into “Adult Nester Equivalents” (ANE) to map retrospective demographic conversions.
  3. Projection: Simulating future population trends over a 100-year evaluation horizon across 8 unique management tracks:
    • Track 1: Pristine baseline (Unimpacted counterfactual with no historical or future fishery take).
    • Track 2: Status Quo (Realized threats under current baseline fishery interaction levels).
    • Track 3: Option A (Nest protection / caging out predators).
    • Track 4: Option B (Juvenile headstarting to yearling releases).
    • Track 5: Option C (Cessation of beach poaching of mature nesting adult females).
    • Track 6: Option D (Gear avoidance/closures to mitigate interaction rates).
    • Track 7: Option E (Safe handling and circle hooks to reduce post-release mortality).
    • Track 8: Combined portfolio (Concurrent implementation of Options A–E).

The primary goals of this project are to (1) expand traditional population assessments to capture the positive impact of diverse conservation actions, and (2) provide an open-access, fully automated workflow for this population assessment method to be easily reproduced and adapted for other species or regions.

R script files to execute the population assessments described here can be found in the GitHub repository for this webpage.
